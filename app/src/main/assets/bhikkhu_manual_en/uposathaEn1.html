PARISUDDHI-UPOSATHA (PURITY OF UPOSATHA)

(i) Parisuddhi before the Sangha

Declaration of one's purity before the Sangha:

"Parisuddho ahaṃ bhante, parisuddho'ti maṃ saṅgho dhāretu."

("Venerable sirs, I am completely pure. May the Sangha regard me as pure.") [cf. Vin,I,129]

 

(ii) Parisuddhi for 3 bhikkhus

At least four bhikkhus are required to recite the Patimokkha. If only three bhikkhus are present, then after performing the preliminary duties and general confession, one bhikkhu pronounces the ñatti:

 

"Suṇantu me bhante, āyasmanto ajj'uposatho pannaraso, yadi āyasmantānaṃ pattakallaṃ, mayam aññamaññaṃ parisuddhi uposathaṃ kareyyāma." ("Let the venerable ones hear me. Today is the Uposatha, the fifteenth day (of the fortnight). If it is convenient for the venerable ones, let us perform the Uposatha in complete purity among ourselves.")

 

  • When it's the 14th day: "pannaraso" → "catuddaso"
  • If the announcing bhikkhu is the most senior: "bhante" → "āvuso"

[cf. Vin,I,124]

 

Then, starting with the most senior bhikkhu:

"Parisuddho ahaṃ āvuso, parisuddho'ti maṃ dhāretha."

("Friends, I am completely pure. Please understand that I am completely pure.")

 

For each of the two junior bhikkhus: "āvuso" → "bhante"

 

(iii) Parisuddhi for 2 bhikkhus

Skip the ñatti. The senior bhikkhu declares purity first:

"Parisuddho ahaṃ āvuso, parisuddho'ti maṃ dhārehi."

("Friend, I am completely pure. Please understand that I am completely pure.")

 

  • For the junior bhikkhu: "āvuso" → "bhante" "dhārehi" → "dhāretha" [cf. Vin,I,124–125]

(iv) Adhicchānuposatha (For one bhikkhu)

For a bhikkhu who is alone on the day of Uposatha. After performing the preliminary duties, he says:

"Ajja me uposatho."

("Today is my Uposatha day.") [cf. Vin,I,125]
