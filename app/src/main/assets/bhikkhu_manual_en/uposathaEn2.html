SICK BHIKKHUS

(I) PARISUDDHI

(a) A sick bhikkhu makes a general confession, then says:

"Pārisuddhiṃ dammi, pārisuddhiṃ me hara, pārisuddhiṃ me ārocehi."

 ("I give my purity. Please convey my purity (and) announce my purity.")

 

*

 

  • If the sick bhikkhu is junior:
  • "hara" → "haratha"; "ārocehi" → "ārocetha" [Vin,I,120]
  •  

(II) SENDING CONSENT (CHANDA)

(a) A sick bhikkhu sends his consent for saṅghakamma:

"Chandaṃ dammi, chandaṃ me hara, chandaṃ me ārocehi."

("I give my consent. Please convey my consent (to the Sangha). Please announce my consent.")

 

*

  • If the sick bhikkhu is junior:
  • "hara" → "haratha"; "ārocehi" → "ārocetha"
    • [Vin,I,121] 

(b) Informing the Sangha about the sick bhikkhu's consent:

"Āyasmā bhante 'Uttaro' mayhaṃ chandaṃ adāsi, tassa chando mayā āhato, sādhu bhante saṅgho dhāretu."

("Venerable sirs, 'Uttaro Bhikkhu' has given me his consent. I have conveyed his consent. May the Sangha acknowledge this, venerable sirs.")

 

  • If the bhikkhu conveying the consent is senior to the sick bhikkhu:
  • "Āyasmā bhante 'Uttaro'" → "'Uttaro' bhante bhikkhu"
    • [Thai; cf. Vin,I,122] 

(III) PARISUDDHI + CHANDA

When both purity and consent are conveyed to the Sangha:

"Uttaro' bhante bhikkhu gilāno mayhaṃ chandañca pārisuddhiñca adāsi, tassa chando ca pārisuddhi ca mayā āhata, sādhu bhante saṅgho dhāretu." ("Venerable sirs, 'Uttaro Bhikkhu' is sick. He has given me his consent and purity. I have conveyed his consent and purity. May the Sangha acknowledge this, venerable sirs.")

[cf. Vin,I,122]
