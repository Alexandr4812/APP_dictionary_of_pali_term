Teaching rule about physical contact

 

translation - Khemanando Bhikkhu

Original story

Once the Buddha stayed in Savatthi in the Jeta grove, in the Anathapindika monastery. At that time, Venerable Udayi was in the wilderness. He had a beautiful home with a room in the middle and corridors on the sides. The bed and bench were beautifully made, and drinking water and bathing water were prepared for use. The courtyards (Parivenas) were well swept. Many people came to see Udaya's home.

among them were a Brahmin and his wife. They approached Udaya and said, “Venerable One, we would like to see the dwelling.”

“Okay Brahmin, please do so.”

Udayi took the key, lifted the latch, opened the door and entered the house. The Brahmin followed him in and then the Brahmin's wife. Opening some windows and closing others, Udayi walked around the interior and came up behind the Brahmin's wife, touching her everywhere. The Brahmin thanked Udaya and left.

And he expressed his delight, “These Saki monks living in the wilderness are excellent. Venerable Udayi is excellent!”

But the Brahmin's wife said, “What is excellent in him? He touched me everywhere like you do."

The Brahmin then complained and criticized him, “These Saki monks are shameless and immoral liars. They claim to have integrity, celibacy and good behavior, truthfulness, morality and goodness. But they do not have the good character of a monk or a Brahmin. They're lost! How could the ascetic Udaya touch my wife everywhere? It is impossible to go to a monastery or monastic residence with a wife from a respected family, or with a daughter, girl, daughter-in-law, or female slave from a respected family. If you do this, the Saki monks may pester them.”

The monks heard about this Brahmin's criticism. Monks with little desire complained and criticized Udayi, “How could Venerable Udayi make physical contact with a woman?”

They told Buddha. He then assembled the Sangha and asked for Udaya.

“Is it true Udaya that you did this?”

"It's true Venerable."

The Buddha rebuked him, “Foolish man, this is not proper, this is not suitable, this is not worthy of a monk, this is not allowed, this should not be done, how could you do this? Have I not given many teachings for the sake of dispassion, and not for the sake of passion... to calm the heat of sensual pleasure? This will affect the confidence of the people..." ... "And monks, this rule should be recited like this:

Final Rule

“If a monk, overcome by lust and with a distorted mind, makes physical contact with a woman—holding her hand or hair, or touching any part of her body—he commits a suspension offense.”

Definitions

Who:

anyone...

Monk:

... A monk who has been given full tonsure by the unanimous Sangha, through a legal procedure consisting of one movement and three announcements, which are irreversible and meet the requirements - this is the type of monk referred to in this case.

Overcome by lust:

having lust, yearning, falling in love.

Distorted:

the lustful mind is distorted; the angry mind is distorted; the confused mind is distorted. But in the case of "distorted" it means the lustful mind.

Woman:

a human woman, not a spirit woman, not a ghost woman, not an animal woman; even a girl born on the same day, not to mention an older one.

WITH:

together.

Makes physical contact:

wrong behavior, that's what it means.

Hand:

From the elbow to the tips of the nails.

Hair:

just hair; or hair with locks in it, with wreaths, with gold coins, with gold, with pearls, or with precious stones.

Any part of her body:

anything other than her hand and hair is called “any part of her body.”

Sub-definitions

Physical contact, touching, stroking down, stroking up, pulling down, lifting, pulling, pushing, squeezing, pressing, holding, contacting.

Physical contact:

only physical contact.

Regarding:

touching here and there.

stroking down:

going down.

stroking up:

rising.

Pulling down:

bending down.

Lifting:

rising.

Pulling:

pulling out.

Pushing:

sending away.

Squeezing:

holding a body part and then applying pressure.

Pressing:

pressing with something.

Holding:

just holding it.

Contacting:

just contact.

He commits a violation entailing suspension:

Hence it is also called a “suspension violation.”

Rearrangements part 2

If, in an effort to connect, he makes an effort with his body and experiences contact, he commits a violation that entails suspension.

If, in an effort to connect, he makes an effort with his body, but does not experience contact, he commits a violation of wrong behavior.

If, striving for connection, he makes no effort with his body, but experiences contact, there is no violation.

If, while striving for connection, he makes no effort with his body and does not experience contact, there is no violation.

If, in an effort to free himself, he makes an effort with his body and experiences contact, there is no violation.

If, in an effort to free himself, he makes an effort with his body, but does not experience contact, there is no violation.

If, in an effort to free himself, he makes no effort with his body, but experiences contact, there is no violation.

If, in trying to free himself, he makes no effort with his body and does not experience contact, there is no violation.

Not a violation

There is no violation if: if it was committed unintentionally; if he is inattentive; if he doesn't know; if he does not agree; if he is crazy; if he is insane; if he is overcome by pain; if he is the first offender.
