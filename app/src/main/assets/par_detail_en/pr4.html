Definitions

Who: any … Monk: ... A monk who has been given full vows by a full Order through a formal procedure consisting of one movement and three announcements that are indisputable and meet the requirements-this type of monk is meant in this case.

False: Although a certain beneficial quality is not present in him, not really, not detectable, and he has not seen it or knows about it, he says, " I have this beneficial quality."

Super-human quality:absorption, liberation, stillness, attainment, knowledge and vision, development of the path, realization of fruits, abandonment of defilements, a mind without hindrance, enjoying in empty dwellings.

For himself: either he presents these good qualities as in himself, or he presents himself as among those good qualities.

Knowledge: the three true insights. Vision: Knowledge and vision are identical.

Applications: Notifies a woman or man, a householder, or a retired homeless person.

This I know, this I see: "I know these qualities/I see these qualities/these qualities are in me and I correspond to them."

After some time: a moment, a second, right after he made the statement.

He is asked: He is asked about what he said: "What have you achieved? How did you achieve this? When did you achieve this? Where did you achieve this? What kind of dirt did you leave behind? What qualities have you achieved?"

Not: He didn't talk to anyone.

Having committed a violation: having bad desires, defeated by desire, claiming a non-existent, invalid superhuman quality, he committed a violation entailing exile.

Desiring purification:he wants to be a householder, a layman, a monastery servant, or a monk who is not fully tonsured.

Without knowing I said I know without seeing it I said I see: "I do not know these qualities/ I do not see these qualities/these qualities are not detectable in me and I do not correspond to them."

What was said is empty and false: what I said was empty; what I said was false; what I said was invalid; I said without knowing.

Except due to revaluation: except if it is a revaluation.

He also: this is said with reference to previous violations leading to expulsion.

Banished[ excluded]: just as a palm tree with its crown cut off is unable to grow any further, so is a monk with bad desires, defeated by desire, who has claimed a non-existent, invalid superhuman quality, not an ascetic, not a son of Sakyas-hence it is said, "he is banished"

Not in the community: Community: common formal procedures, same recitation, same training-this is called a " community."
