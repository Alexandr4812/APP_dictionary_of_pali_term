52 Cetasikas
Ethically Variable (13)
Universals (7)
1
Contact (Phassa)
The touching of consciousness and factors with an object.
2
Feeling (Vedanā)
Experiencing the 'taste' of an object (pleasant, unpleasant, neutral).
3
Perception (Saññā)
Marking the features of an object for future recognition.
4
Volition (Cetanā)
Intention that coordinates factors; the essence of kamma.
5
One-pointedness (Ekaggatā)
Focusing on a single object; the seed of concentration.
6
Life Faculty (Jīvitindriya)
Sustaining the vitality of mental states.
7
Attention (Manasikāra)
Turning the mind towards the object. Directing associated factors to the object.
Occasionals (6)
8
Initial Application (Vitakka)
Directing the mind towards the object.
9
Sustained Application (Vicāra)
Continued engagement of the mind with the object.
10
Decision (Adhimokkha)
Making a decision, absence of hesitation.
11
Energy (Viriya)
Effort that supports wholesome and unwholesome states.
12
Zest (Pīti)
Joyful interest, inspiration (refreshing body and mind).
13
Desire-to-act (Chanda)
The wish to act or achieve (neutral).
Unwholesome Factors (14)
Universals (4)
14
Delusion (Moha)
Blindness of the mind to the true nature of things.
15
Shamelessness (Ahirika)
Lack of aversion to wrongdoing.
16
Fearlessness of wrong (Anottappa)
Lack of fear of the consequences of evil.
17
Restlessness (Uddhacca)
Unsettledness, agitation of the mind.
Occasionals (10)
18
Greed (Lobha)
Attachment, craving for sensual objects.
19
Wrong View (Diṭṭhi)
Distorted perception of reality (belief in a "self," etc.).
20
Conceit (Māna)
Pride, comparing oneself to others.
21
Hatred (Dosa)
Irritation, anger, hostility, aversion.
22
Envy (Issā)
Jealousy of others' success.
23
Stinginess (Macchariya)
Desire to hide something from others, not to share with others.
24
Worry / Regret (Kukkucca)
Anxiety about evil done or good not done.
25
Sloth (Thīna)
Rigidity, heaviness of mental factors.
26
Torpor (Middha)
Drowsiness, dullness of consciousness itself.
27
Doubt (Vicikicchā)
Skeptical doubt in the Buddha, Dhamma, and the Path.
Beautiful Factors (25)
Universals (19)
28
Faith (Saddhā)
Confidence in enlightenment and truth.
29
Mindfulness (Sati)
Recollection, the mind not drifting from the object. Non-wavering (presence of mind, non-forgetting).
30
Shame (Hiri)
Aversion to committing evil.
31
Fear of wrong (Ottappa)
Moral fear of bad consequences.
32
Non-greed (Alobha)
Generosity, lack of attachment.
33
Non-hatred (Adosa)
Absence of cruelty (non-opposition).
34
Neutrality (Tatramajjhattatā)
Equanimity, balance of mental states.
35-36
Tranquility (Passaddhi)
Calmness of the mental body and consciousness.
37-38
Lightness (Lahutā)
Absence of heaviness and sluggishness. Alleviation of heaviness in factors.
39-40
Malleability (Mudutā)
Plasticity, removal of rigidity in factors and body.
41-42
Wieldiness (Kammaññatā)
Workability, suitability for practice. Removal of unwieldiness in factors.
43-44
Proficiency (Pāguññatā)
Mastery, absence of mental "sickness".
45-46
Rectitude (Ujjukatā)
Honesty and sincerity of mind.
Abstinences (3)
47
Right Speech (Sammā-vācā)
Abstaining from lying, slander, harsh speech, and idle chatter.
48
Right Action (Sammā-kammanta)
Abstaining from killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct.
49
Right Livelihood (Sammā-ājīva)
Engaging in activities that do not harm beings.
Illimitables (2)
50
Compassion (Karuṇā)
Contributing to the elimination of suffering in others.
51
Sympathetic Joy (Muditā)
Rejoicing in the success and happiness of others.
Wisdom (1)
52
Wisdom (Paññindriya)
Insightful understanding of the Four Noble Truths.
