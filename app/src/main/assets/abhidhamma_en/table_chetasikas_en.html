52 Cetasikas
Ethically Variable (13)
Universals (7)
1
Contact (Phassa)
Ch: touching; F: collision (of object with door); M: concurrence (of consciousness, object, and base); PC: object that has come into focus.
2
Feeling (Vedanā)
Ch: experiencing (tasting); F: experiencing the impact of the object; M: relishing; PC: contact.
3
Perception (Saññā)
Ch: noting (recognizing); F: creating a sign (mark); M: interpretation by means of the sign; PC: manifested object.
4
Volition (Cetanā)
Ch: state of willing; F: accumulation (of kamma); M: coordination of associated states; PC: associated states.
5
One-pointedness (Ekaggatā)
Ch: non-distraction; F: unifying associated states; M: peace (calm); PC: happiness.
6
Life faculty (Jīvitindriya)
Ch: maintaining mental states; F: causing them to occur; M: establishing their presence; PC: states to be maintained.
7
Attention (Manasikāra)
Ch: directing (leading) factors to the object; F: attaching factors to the object; M: confrontation with the object; PC: object.
Occasionals (6)
8
Initial application (Vitakka)
Ch: directing the mind onto the object; F: striking and threshing the object; M: leading the mind to the object; PC: object.
9
Sustained application (Vicāra)
Ch: occupancy (keeping) of the mind on the object; F: threshing (anchoring); M: anchoring the mind on the object; PC: object.
10
Decision (Adhimokkha)
Ch: conviction; F: non-probing (absence of wavering); M: decisiveness; PC: something to be convinced about.
11
Energy (Viriya)
Ch: state of exertion (effort); F: supporting associated states; M: non-collapse (steadfastness); PC: sense of urgency.
12
Rapture (Pīti)
Ch: endearing (refreshing); F: pervading body and mind with rapture; M: elation (enthusiasm); PC: mind-matter.
13
Desire (Chanda)
Ch: desire to act; F: searching for the object; M: need for the object; PC: object.
Unwholesome Factors (14)
Universals (4)
14
Delusion (Moha)
Ch: mental blindness (ignorance); F: concealing the true nature of the object; M: absence of right seeing; PC: unwise attention.
15
Shamelessness (Ahirika)
Ch: absence of disgust at evil; F: committing transgressions; M: non-shrinking from evil; PC: lack of self-respect.
16
Fearlessness of wrong (Anottappa)
Ch: absence of fear of consequences; F: committing transgressions; M: non-shrinking from evil; PC: lack of respect for others.
17
Restlessness (Uddhacca)
Ch: agitation (like water whipped by wind); F: instability; M: confusion; PC: unwise attention to agitation.
Occasionals (10)
18
Greed (Lobha)
Ch: grasping the object; F: sticking (like meat to a hot pan); M: non-relinquishing; PC: seeing enjoyment in things.
19
Wrong view (Diṭṭhi)
Ch: unwise interpretation; F: assuming the false; M: wrong conviction; PC: unwillingness to see the noble ones.
20
Conceit (Māna)
Ch: haughtiness (arrogance); F: self-exaltation; M: vainglory; PC: greed without views.
21
Hatred (Dosa)
Ch: ferocity (fierceness); F: burning one's own support; M: persecuting; PC: basis for irritation.
22
Envy (Issā)
Ch: jealousy of others' success; F: discontent with others' success; M: aversion to others' success; PC: others' success.
23
Stinginess (Macchariya)
Ch: concealing one's own success; F: intolerance of sharing with others; M: meanness (baseness); PC: one's own success.
24
Worry / Remorse (Kukkucca)
Ch: subsequent regret; F: grieving over what was done/left undone; M: remorse; PC: errors of commission and omission.
25
Sloth (Thīna)
Ch: lack of driving force; F: dispelling energy; M: decline (dullness) of mind; PC: unwise attention to boredom.
26
Torpor (Middha)
Ch: unwieldiness (sickness of factors); F: oppression; M: drowsiness (nodding); PC: laziness.
27
Doubt (Vicikicchā)
Ch: doubtfulness (wavering); F: inability to trust; M: indecision; PC: unwise attention.
Beautiful Factors (25)
Universals (19)
28
Faith (Saddhā)
Ch: placing trust; F: purifying (like a gem clarifies muddy water); M: resolution (clarity); PC: object of faith.
29
Mindfulness (Sati)
Ch: non-wavering (presence of mind); F: absence of confusion; M: guarding (confrontation with object); PC: strong perception.
30
Moral shame (Hiri)
Ch: disgust at evil; F: non-committing of evil; M: shrinking from evil; PC: self-respect.
31
Moral dread (Ottappa)
Ch: fear of evil; F: non-committing of evil; M: fear of consequences; PC: respect for others.
32
Non-greed (Alobha)
Ch: absence of attachment; F: non-grasping; M: non-adherence; PC: wise attention.
33
Non-hatred (Adosa)
Ch: absence of ferocity (meekness); F: removing hostility; M: agreeableness; PC: wise attention.
34
Neutrality (Tatramajjhattatā)
Ch: balance; F: preventing excess/deficiency; M: impartial contemplation; PC: associated states.
35-36
Tranquility (Passaddhi)
Ch: calming of disturbances (of factors/consciousness); F: destroying disturbances; M: peacefulness; PC: factors/consciousness.
37-38
Lightness (Lahutā)
Ch: weakening of heaviness; F: unloading heaviness; M: absence of inertia; PC: factors/consciousness.
39-40
Pliancy (Mudutā)
Ch: removal of stiffness; F: destroying stiffness; M: non-resistance; PC: factors/consciousness.
41-42
Wieldiness (Kammaññatā)
Ch: removal of unwieldiness; F: destroying unwieldiness; M: success in receiving the object; PC: factors/consciousness.
43-44
Proficiency (Pāguññatā)
Ch: healthiness (of factors/consciousness); F: destroying unhealthiness; M: absence of disability; PC: factors/consciousness.
45-46
Uprightness (Ujjukatā)
Ch: honesty (straightness); F: destroying crookedness; M: non-deviousness; PC: factors/consciousness.

49

Illimitables (2)
50
Compassion (Karuṇā)
Ch: promoting the removal of suffering in others; F: inability to bear others' suffering; M: non-cruelty; PC: seeing helplessness in those who suffer.
51
Appreciative joy (Muditā)
Ch: rejoicing in others' success; F: non-enviousness; M: removal of aversion (boredom); PC: seeing others' success.
Wisdom (1)
52
Wisdom (Paññindriya)
Ch: penetrating the true nature (knowledge); F: illuminating (removing the darkness of ignorance); M: absence of confusion; PC: concentration.
