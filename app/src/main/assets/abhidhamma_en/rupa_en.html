28 Rūpa — Material Phenomena
Concretely Produced Matter (18)
I. The Four Great Elements (4)
1
Earth Element (Pathavī)
The element of solidity and extension; the foundation for the other elements.
2
Water Element (Āpo)
The element of fluidity and cohesion; binds the other elements together.
3
Fire Element (Tejo)
The element of heat; maintains temperature and supports maturation.
4
Air Element (Vāyo)
The element of motion and pressure; generates movement.
II. Sense Phenomena (5)
5
Eye Sensitivity (Cakkhu-pasāda)
The sensitive matter of the eye that receives visible objects.
6
Ear Sensitivity (Sota-pasāda)
The sensitive matter of the ear that receives sound.
7
Nose Sensitivity (Ghāna-pasāda)
The sensitive matter of the nose that receives odours.
8
Tongue Sensitivity (Jivhā-pasāda)
The sensitive matter of the tongue that receives tastes.
9
Body Sensitivity (Kāya-pasāda)
The sensitive matter of the body that receives tactile objects.
III. Object Phenomena (4)
10
Visible Form (Rūpa)
The object of sight — colour and visible shape.
11
Sound (Sadda)
The object of hearing — sound waves.
12
Odour (Gandha)
The object of smell — scent molecules.
13
Taste (Rasa)
The object of taste. Tangibility (= 3 elements: earth, fire, air) is not counted as a separate phenomenon.
IV. Sex Phenomena (2)
14
Femininity (Itthibhāva)
The material phenomenon that determines the female nature of the body.
15
Masculinity (Pumbhāva)
The material phenomenon that determines the male nature of the body.
V. Heart Phenomenon (1)
16
Heart-Base (Hadaya-vatthu)
The material base of the mind — the physical substrate for mental consciousness.
VI. Life Phenomenon (1)
17
Controlling Life-Faculty (Jīvitindriya)
Maintains the other material phenomena in a living state.
VII. Nutrition Phenomenon (1)
18
Nutrition (Āhāra / Ojā)
The nutritive essence (ojā) in food that sustains the material body.
Abstractly Produced Matter (10)
VIII. Delimiting Phenomenon (1)
19
Space Element (Ākāsa-dhātu)
Space (void) that delimits and separates groups of material phenomena.
IX. Communication Phenomena (2)
20
Bodily Communication (Kāya-viññatti)
Bodily movement expressing intention — gestures and facial expressions.
21
Verbal Communication (Vacī-viññatti)
Speech sound expressing intention — words and language.
X. Mutability Phenomena (3)
22
Lightness (Rūpassa lahutā)
Lightness of matter — absence of heaviness and sluggishness. (Includes the two types of communication)
23
Softness (Rūpassa mudutā)
Pliability of matter — removal of rigidity.
24
Wieldiness (Rūpassa kammaññatā)
Workability of matter — fitness for activity. (Plus the two types of communication)
XI. Characteristics of Matter (4)
25
Production (Upacaya)
Initial accumulation and growth of material phenomena.
26
Continuity (Santati)
The ongoing stream of material phenomena without interruption.
27
Decay (Jaratā)
Ageing and deterioration of material phenomena.
28
Impermanence (Aniccatā)
Transience — the cessation of material phenomena.
