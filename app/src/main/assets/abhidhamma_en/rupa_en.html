28 Rūpa — Material Phenomena
Really Produced Matter (18)
I. Great Elements (4)
1
Earth element (Pathavī)
Ch: solidity (hardness); F: acting as foundation for co-arising matter; M: assuming or acquiring shape; PC: the other three great elements.
2
Water element (Āpo)
Ch: fluidity (oozing); F: cohesion (binding) of material particles; M: holding together; PC: the other three great elements.
3
Fire element (Tejo)
Ch: heat (warmth) or cold; F: maturing (ripening); M: source of softness; PC: the other three great elements.
4
Air element (Vāyo)
Ch: distension (pressure); F: causing motion; M: conveying to other places; PC: the other three great elements.
II. Sensory Matter (5)
5
Eye sensitivity (Cakkhu-pasāda)
Ch: readiness to be impacted by visible data; F: apprehending the object; M: support for eye-consciousness; PC: kamma born from desire to see.
6
Ear sensitivity (Sota-pasāda)
Ch: readiness to be impacted by sounds; F: apprehending sound; M: support for ear-consciousness; PC: kamma born from desire to hear.
7
Nose sensitivity (Ghāna-pasāda)
Ch: readiness to be impacted by odours; F: apprehending smell; M: support for nose-consciousness; PC: kamma born from desire to smell.
8
Tongue sensitivity (Jivhā-pasāda)
Ch: readiness to be impacted by tastes; F: apprehending taste; M: support for tongue-consciousness; PC: kamma born from desire to taste.
9
Body sensitivity (Kāya-pasāda)
Ch: readiness to be impacted by tangibles; F: apprehending the tangible; M: support for body-consciousness; PC: kamma born from desire to touch.
III. Objective Matter (4)
10
Visible form (Rūpa)
Ch: impacting the eye; F: being the object of sight; M: refuge for eye-consciousness; PC: 4 great elements.
11
Sound (Sadda)
Ch: impacting the ear; F: being the object of hearing; M: domain of ear-consciousness; PC: 4 great elements.
12
Smell (Gandha)
Ch: impacting the nose; F: being the object of smell; M: domain of nose-consciousness; PC: 4 great elements.
13
Taste (Rasa)
Ch: impacting the tongue; F: being the object of taste; M: domain of tongue-consciousness; PC: 4 great elements.
IV. Sex Matter (2)
14
Femininity (Itthibhāva)
Ch: female sex; F: showing that "this is a woman"; M: cause for feminine features and manners; PC: 4 great elements.
15
Masculinity (Pumbhāva)
Ch: male sex; F: showing that "this is a man"; M: cause for masculine features and manners; PC: 4 great elements.
V. Heart Matter (1)
16
Heart-base (Hadaya-vatthu)
Ch: material support for the mind; F: supporting mental elements; M: carrying (receptacle) of these elements; PC: 4 great elements and blood.
VI. Life Matter (1)
17
Life faculty (Jīvitindriya)
Ch: maintaining co-arisen matter; F: making it occur; M: establishing the presence of matter; PC: 4 great elements.
VII. Nutriment Matter (1)
18
Nutriment (Āhāra / Ojā)
Ch: nutritive essence; F: sustaining the body; M: strengthening the body; PC: edible food.
Indirectly Produced Matter (10)
VIII. Delimiting Matter (1)
19
Space element (Ākāsa-dhātu)
Ch: delimiting matter; F: showing the boundaries of material groups; M: state of openings and intervals; PC: delimited matter.
IX. Communicating Matter (2)
20
Bodily communication (Kāya-viññatti)
F: manifesting intention through movements; M: cause of bodily movements; PC: consciousness-born air element.
21
Verbal communication (Vacī-viññatti)
F: manifesting intention through speech; M: cause of verbal utterances; PC: consciousness-born earth element.
X. Mutable Matter (3)
22
Lightness (Rūpassa lahutā)
Ch: absence of unwieldiness; F: dispelling heaviness; M: easy transformability; PC: light matter.
23
Softness (Rūpassa mudutā)
Ch: absence of stiffness; F: dispelling rigidity; M: non-resistance to action; PC: pliable matter.
24
Wieldiness (Rūpassa kammaññatā)
Ch: suitability for action; F: dispelling unwieldiness; M: absence of weakness; PC: manageable matter.
XI. Characteristics of Matter (4)
25
Production (Upacaya)
Ch: establishing the process of arising; F: initiating the arising of material groups; M: completed state at first arising; PC: produced matter.
26
Continuity (Santati)
Ch: ability to occur repeatedly; F: consolidation (continuity); M: uninterrupted process; PC: matter subject to consolidation.
27
Decay (Jaratā)
Ch: maturing; F: leading phenomena to dissolution; M: loss of newness; PC: maturing matter.
28
Impermanence (Aniccatā)
Ch: complete dissolution; F: cessation of phenomena; M: destruction and vanishing; PC: dissolved matter.
