**Confession of a Junior Monk to a Senior Monk**

 

**Junior Confessing Monk (JCM):**

 

“Okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.

Dutiyam-pi ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.

Tatiyam-pi ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.”

 

("Venerable sir, I confess all offenses. A second time... A third time...")

 

**Senior Confirming Monk (SCM):**

 

“Sadhu, sadhu.”

(“Good, good.”)

 

**JCM:**

 

“Okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Sambahulā nānā vatthukā nānā apattiyo apajjiṁ,

Tā tumha-mūle paṭidesemi.”

 

("Venerable sir, I have committed various offenses on different grounds, and I confess them before you.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Passasi āvuso tā apattiyo?”

("Friend, do you see these offenses?")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Āma bhante passāmi.”

("Yes, venerable sir, I see.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Āyatiṁ āvuso saṁvareyyāsi.”

("In the future, friend, you should be more mindful.")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.

Dutiyam-pi sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.

Tatiyam-pi sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.”

 

("That is indeed good, venerable sir, I will restrain myself in the future. A second time... A third time...")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Sadhu, sadhu.”

("Good, good.")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā tā garukāpattiyo āvikaromi.

Dutiyam-pi okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā tā garukāpattiyo āvikaromi.

Tatiyam-pi okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Sabbā tā garukāpattiyo āvikaromi.”

 

("Venerable sir, I disclose all grave offenses. A second time... A third time...")

 

This concluding statement is used only in some communities. Additionally, in some communities, “sadhu” is recited after every statement, not as shown above (i.e., after every "ārocemi" and every "saṁvarissāmi").

**Confession of a Senior Monk to a Junior Monk**

 

**Senior Confessing Monk (SCM):**

 

“Ahaṁ, āvuso, sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.

Dutiyam-pi sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.

Tatiyam-pi sabbā apāttiyo ārocemi.”

 

("Friend, I confess all offenses. A second time... A third time...")

 

**Junior Confirming Monk (JCM):**

 

“Sadhu, sadhu.”

("Good, good.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Okāsa, ahaṁ avuso,

Sambahulā nānā-vatthukā nānā apattiyo apajjim,

Tā tumha-mūle paṭidesemi.”

 

("Friend, I have committed various offenses on different grounds, and I confess them before you.")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Passatha bhante tā apattiyo?”

("Venerable sir, do you see these offenses?")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Āma avuso passāmi.”

("Yes, friend, I see.")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Āyatim bhante samvareyyatha.”

("In the future, venerable sir, you should be more mindful.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Sādhu suṭṭhu avuso āyatim samvarissāmi.

Dutiyam-pi suṭṭhu samvarissāmi.

Tatiyam-pi suṭṭhu samvarissāmi.”

 

("Good, friend, I will restrain myself in the future. A second time... A third time...")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Sadhu, sadhu.”

("Good, good.")

**If Both Monks Have the Same Offenses**

 

**JCM:**

 

“Okāsa, ahaṁ bhante,

Desanādukkaṭāpattiṁ āpajjiṁ,

Taṁ tumha-mūle paṭidesemi.”

 

("Venerable sir, I admit my offense, having confessed the same offenses.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Passasi āvuso taṁ apattiṁ?”

("Friend, do you see this offense?")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Āma bhante passāmi.”

("Yes, venerable sir, I see.")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Āyatiṁ āvuso saṁvareyyāsi.”

("In the future, friend, you should be more mindful.")

 

**JCM:**

 

“Sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.

Dutiyam-pi sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.

Tatiyam-pi sādhu suṭṭhu bhante āyatiṁ saṁvarissāmi.”

 

("That is indeed good, venerable sir, in the future I will restrain myself. A second time... A third time...")

 

**SCM:**

 

“Sadhu, sadhu.”

("Good, good.")

(i) **Six Causes of an Offense (āpatthi):**

 

Lack of shame; ignorance of the rules; doubt, but proceeding; thinking something should be done even though it shouldn’t; thinking something shouldn't be done but doing it; acting carelessly (i.e., absentmindedly).

 

(ii) **No Offense Occurs for:**

 

Insane monks, delirious monks, those suffering from severe pain, or original transgressors.

 

(iii) **Types of Offenses (āpatthi):**

 

(a) **Irremediable Offenses** (pārājika).

(b) **Remediable Offenses:**

 

- Serious offenses (sanghādisesa), confessed to the Sangha.

- Minor offenses, confessed to another monk:

 

  **thullaccaya** (serious offenses),

  **pācittiya** (offenses requiring expiation),

  **pati-desanīya** (offenses requiring confession),

  **dukkata** (wrong actions),

  **dubbhāsita** (wrong speech).

 

(iv) **Method of Confession for Minor Offenses:**

 

Before confessing, any known offenses should be stated. Two monks who have committed the same offense should not confess to each other. This is a dukkata offense.

[Vin. IV,122]

 

First, the junior monk confesses, recalling various offenses. Then the senior monk does the same.
