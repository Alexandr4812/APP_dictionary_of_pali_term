RECITATION AFTER EVENING MEDITATION
(At Cittaviveka Monastery)




Imāya dhammānudhammapaṭipattiyā Buddhaṃ pūjemi.
May this practice be an offering to the Buddha.

Imāya dhammānudhammapaṭipattiyā dhammam pūjemi.
May this practice be an offering to the Dhamma.

Imāya dhammānudhammapaṭipattiyā Saṅghaṃ pūjemi.
May this practice be an offering to the Sangha.

Addhā imāya patipattiyā jāti jarā vyādhi maraṇamhā parimuccissāmi.
May this practice lead to liberation from birth, aging, illness, and death.

Idam me punnam kamma asavekkhaya vaham hotu.
May this wholesome kamma of mine lead to the destruction of the āsavas.

Sabbe dukha pamojjatu.
May all suffering and pleasure...

 

Jayo munindassa subodhi mūle
"Victory for the Buddha beneath the great Bodhi tree

ahosi mārassa parajāyosi
was the cause of Māra's defeat!"

ugghosayuṃ devagaṇā pasannā
Various deities exclaimed joyfully.

etena saccena jayantu tuyhaṃ.
By this truth, may you be victorious.

 

Sabbe buddhā balappattā paccekānañca yaṃ balaṃ arahantānañca tejena rakkhaṃ bandhāmi sabbaso.
By the power of all Buddhas, by the strength of all Paccekabuddhas, by the might of the Arahants, may I be fully protected in every way.

 

Ākāsatthā ca bhummaṭṭhā dēvā nāgā mahiddhikā
May the gods and Nāgas, dwelling in the sky and on earth,

Puññaṃ tam anumōditvā ciram rakkhantu lōka sāsanaṃ.
rejoice in our merit and protect the Buddha’s dispensation for a long time.

Ākāsatthā ca bhummaṭṭhā dēvā nāgā mahiddhikā
May the gods and Nāgas, dwelling in the sky and on earth,

Puññaṃ tam anumōditvā ciram rakkhantu dēsanaṃ.
rejoice in our merit and protect the Dhamma teachings for a long time.

Ākāsatthā ca bhummaṭṭhā dēvā nāgā mahiddhikā
May the gods and Nāgas, dwelling in the sky and on earth,

Puññaṃ tam anumōditvā ciram rakkhantu maṃ paraṃ.
rejoice in our merit and protect me and others for a long time.

 

Iminā puññakammēna - mā me bālasamāgamō
By this meritorious action, may I never associate with fools.

Sataṃ samāgamō hōtu - yāva nibbāna pattiyā.
May I always associate with the wise until I reach Nibbāna.

Idaṃ me puññaṃ - āsavakkhayā vahaṃ hōtu.
May this merit lead me to the destruction of the āsavas.

Sabba dukkhā pamuccatu.
May all suffering disappear.

 

Idaṃ no (me) ñātīnaṃ hōtu - sukhitā hontu ñātayō [3x].
May this be for the benefit of our (my) relatives. May my relatives be happy.

 

Dēvo vassatu kālena
May the rains fall in due season.

Sassasampatti hētu ca
May the crops yield abundantly.

Phito bhavātu lōko ca
May the world be prosperous.

Raja bhavatu dhammiko
May the government be righteous.
