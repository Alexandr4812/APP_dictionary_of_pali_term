Abhivādana-sīlissa—Niccaṁ vuḍḍhāpacāyino


For those who constantly show respect for morality and those who deserve veneration,

 

Cattāro dhammā vaḍḍhanti—Āyu vaṇṇo sukhaṁ balaṁ


Four qualities increase: longevity, beauty, happiness, and strength.

 

Ayu raro ge sampatti


I wish you longevity and freedom from illness.

 

Sage sampatti me vacha


May you be reborn in the heavenly realms.

 

Ato nibbāna sampatti, Imina te samijjhatu


May you one day attain Nibbāna!

 

 

Commentary:

Once, two ascetics lived together and practiced austerity. Later, one of them returned to lay life and got married. When he and his wife had a son, they brought the boy to the other ascetic. The ascetic said, "Live long!" to the parents but did not say anything to the child. The parents were surprised and asked him why he did this. The ascetic replied that through his own powers, he had seen that the boy would die in seven days. He sent them to see the Buddha, who might know how to prevent the child's death.

When they approached the Buddha, he also said, "Live long!" only to the parents and nothing to the son. To prevent the boy's death, he instructed the parents to build a pavilion near their house and place the child on a couch inside it. Then he sent several monks to chant protective verses. Many gods and other celestial beings came to the pavilion and were present there.

On the seventh day, the Buddha himself arrived. Then an evil demon came to the pavilion and stood at the entrance, waiting for an opportunity to attack the boy. But since there were so many powerful beings around, he could not get close to him. The chanting continued throughout the night, and in the morning, the demon had to leave. The child was saved.

The boy then paid homage to the Buddha, who said to him, "Live long!" Many years passed, and the boy grew up. One day, he approached the Buddha and asked him how to gain longevity. The Buddha responded with this verse, saying that those who show respect to virtuous people and pay homage to those worthy of respect will gain not only longevity but also beauty, happiness, and strength.
