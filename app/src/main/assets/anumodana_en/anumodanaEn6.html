BLESSINGS TO LAY PEOPLE WHEN TAKING DANA

 

Abhivādana-sīlissa—Niccaṁ vuḍḍhāpacāyino;

For those who constantly show respect to morality and those who should be honored

 

Cattāro dhammā vaḍḍhanti—Āyu vaṇṇo sukhaṁ balaṁ.

May four dhammas increase: longevity, beauty, happiness, strength

 

Ayu raro ge sampatti,

I wish you longevity and absence of diseases.

 

Sage sampatti me vacha,

May you have rebirth in the heavens.

 

Ato nibbana sampatti, Imina te samijjhatu.

May you one day attain Nibbana!

 

Commentary:

Once, two ascetics lived together and practiced austerities. Later, one of them returned to worldly life and got married. When he and his wife had a son, they took the boy to the other ascetic. He said, "Live long!" to the parents, but said nothing to the child. The parents were surprised and asked him why he did this. The ascetic said that through his powers he saw that the boy would die in just seven days. He sent them to see the Buddha, who might know how to prevent his death. When they approached the Buddha, he again said, "Live long!" only to the parents and nothing to the son. To prevent his death, he told the parents to build a pavilion at their house and place the child on a couch in it. Then he sent several monks to recite protective texts. Many gods and other celestial beings came to the pavilion and were present. On the seventh day, the Buddha himself arrived. Then an evil demon came to the pavilion and stood at the entrance. He was waiting for an opportunity to attack the boy. But because there were so many powerful beings around, he couldn't get close to him. The chanting continued all night, and in the morning the demon had to leave. The little child was saved.

Then he expressed his respect to the Buddha, who said to him, "Live long!" Many years passed and the boy grew up. One day he came to the Buddha and asked him how to attain longevity.

The Buddha responded with this verse, saying that those who respect people with virtuous character and show reverence to those worthy of honor will gain not only longevity, but also beauty, happiness, and strength.
