AṬṬHAVĪSATI BUDDHĀ
“Twenty-eight Buddhas.”

TAṆHAṄKARO MAHĀVĪRO
“Taṇhaṅkara, the great hero.”

MEDHAṄKARO MAHĀYASO
“Medhaṅkara, possessed of great fame.”

SARAṆAṄKARO LOKAHITO
“Saraṇaṅkara, the benefactor of the world”
(loka-hita: “the welfare of the world”).

DĪPAṄKARO JUTINDHARO
“Dīpaṅkara, the bearer of radiance”
(juti-indhara/jutindhara: “one who carries light”).

KOṆḌAÑÑO JANAPĀMOKKHO
“Koṇḍañña, foremost among people/outstanding in the land”
(janapāmokkha: “preeminent among the people”).

MAṄGALO PURISĀSABHO
“Maṅgala, the bull among men”
(purisāsabha: an epithet meaning “best among men”).

SUMANO SUMANO DHĪRO
“Sumana, well-disposed in mind, a steadfast sage”
(dhīra: “steadfast, wise”).

REVATO RATIVADDHANO
“Revata, increaser of delight” (rati-vaddhana).

SOBHITO GUṆASAMPANNO
“Sobhita, endowed with virtues” (guṇa-sampanna).

ANOMADASSĪ JANUTTAMO
“Anomadassī, the highest among people” (jana-uttama).

PADUMO LOKAPAJJOTO
“Paduma, the light of the world.”

NĀRADO VARASĀRATHĪ
“Nārada, the best charioteer”
(vara-sārathi: “the best charioteer,” a metaphor for a guide of beings (to Nibbāna)).

PADUMUTTARO SATTASĀRO
“Padumuttara, essential among beings”
(satta-sāra: “the core/essence of beings”; “one whose essence is a support for the living”).

SUMEDHO AGGAPUGGALO
“Sumedha, the supreme person.”

SUJĀTO SABBALOKAGGO
“Sujāta, the foremost in all the world.”

PIYADASSĪ NARĀSABHO
“Piyadassī, the bull among men.”

ATTHADASSĪ KĀRUṆIKO
“Atthadassī, imbued with compassion”
(kāruṇika: “compassionate”).

DHAMMADASSĪ TAMONUDO
“Dhammadassī, dispeller of darkness”
(tamo-nuda: “one who drives away darkness”).

SIDDHATTHO ASAMO LOKE
“Siddhattha, unequalled in the world”
(asama: “without equal”).

TISSO VARADA SAṂVARO
“Tissa, bestower of excellent restraint,” or “Tissa, who grants supreme self-restraint” (vara-da saṃvara).

PHUSSO VARADA SAMBUDDHO
“Phussa, a Sammasambuddha, giver of the highest good.”

VIPASSĪ CA ANŪPAMO
“Vipassī, incomparable.”

SIKHĪ SABBAHITOSATTO
“Sikhī, a being for the welfare of all”
(sabba-hita-satta: “one whose nature is the good of all”).

VESSABHŪ SUKHADĀYAKO
“Vessabhū, giver of happiness.”

KAKUSANDHO SATTHAVĀHO
“Kakusandha, leader of the caravan”
(sattha-vāha: leader of a caravan, a metaphor for the Teacher guiding the community).

KOṆĀGAMANO RAṆAÑJAHO
“Koṇāgamana, remover of the stain of conflict/enmity” (raṇa-ñjaha/raṇañjaha: “one who removes the defilement of strife”; or in sense “one who abandons defilements/hostility”).

KASSAPO SIRISAMPANNO
“Kassapa, endowed with glory and auspicious prosperity”
(siri-sampanna: “possessed of grace/fortune/well-being”).

GOTAMO SAKYAPUṄGAVO
“Gotama, eminent in the Sakyan clan”
(sakya-puṅgava: “the foremost/bull of the Sakyans”).

TESAṂ SACCENA SĪLENA KHANTI-METTA-BALENA CA TEPI/MAṂ
ANURAKKHANTU ĀROGYENA SUKHENA CĀTI.
“By their truth, virtue, patience, and the power of loving-kindness, may they protect you/me with health and happiness.”
