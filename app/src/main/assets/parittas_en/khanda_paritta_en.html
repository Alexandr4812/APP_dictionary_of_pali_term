K H A N D H A P A R I T T A
Khandha-paritta

Evaṃ me sutaṃ,
So I have heard,

Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapindikassa ārāme.
Once the Blessed One stayed in Savatthi, in Jetavana, in the Anathapindikassa Park.

Tena kho pana samayena sāvatthiyaṃ aññataro bhikkhu ahinā daṭṭho kālakato hoti.
At that time, in Savatthi, a certain monk who had been bitten by a snake died.

Atha kho, sambahulā bhikkhu yena bhagavā tenupasankamiṃsu.
Then many monks came to where the Blessed One was.

Upasankamitvā bhagavantaṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisidiṃsu.
Approaching, they bowed to the Blessed One and sat down on the side.

Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te bhikkhu bhagavantaṃ etadavocuṃ.
Sitting aside, those monks said to the Blessed One:

Ida bhante, sāvatthiyaṃ, aññataro bhikkhu ahinā daṭṭho kālakato ti.
Here, Venerable One, in Savatthi, a certain monk, bitten by a snake, died.

Na ha nūna so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cattāri ahirājakulāni mettena cittena phari.
It is true, monks, that the monk must not have extended the mind of benevolence (metta) to the four kinds of serpent kings.

Sace hi so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu cattāri ahirājakūlāni mettena cittena phareyya,
If, monks, that monk had extended the mind of benevolence to the four kinds of serpent kings,

na hi so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ahinā daṭṭho kālaṃ kareyya.
then, monks, if he had been bitten by a snake, he would not have died.

Katamāni cattāri ahirājakulāni?
What are the four kinds of serpent kings?

Virūpakkhaṃ ahirājakulaṃ;
The lineage of the Serpent Kings of Virūpakkha;

Erāpathaṃ ahirājakulaṃ,
The lineage of the serpent kings of Erāpatha,

Chabyāputtaṃ ahirājakulaṃ,
The lineage of the Serpent Kings of Chabbyaputta,

Kanhāgotamakaṃ ahirājakulaṃ.
The lineage of the Serpent Kings of Kanhāgotamaka.

Na ha nūna, so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu imāni cattāri ahirājakulāni mettena cittena phari.
True, monks, that monk must not have extended the mind of benevolence to these four kinds of serpent kings.

Sace hi, so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu imāni cattāri ahirājakulāni mettena cittena phareyya,
If, monks, that monk extended a benevolent mind to these four kinds of snake kings,

na hi so, bhikkhave, bhikkhu ahinā daṭṭho kālaṃ kareyya.
then, monks, if he had been bitten by a snake, he would not have died.

Anujānāmi, bhikkhave, imāni cattāri ahirājakulāni mettena cittena pharituṃ,
I allow you, monks, to extend your minds with benevolence to these four kinds of king-serpents,

attaguttiyā,
to protect oneself,

attarakkhāya,
to protect oneself,

attaparittāyā ti.
for one's own enclosure (paritta).

Idam avoca bhagavā
So said the Blessed One,

Idaṃ vatvā sugato athāparaṃ etadavoca satthā;
Having said this, Sugata went on to say, Master:

Virūpakkhehi me mettaṃ —
May I have metta for Virupakkham,

mettaṃ erāpathehi me
may I have metta for the Erapathams,

chabyāputtehi me mettaṃ —
may I have metta for the Chabbyaputts,

mettaṃ kanhāgotamakehi ca
and may I have metta for the Kanhagotamakes.

Apādakehi me mettaṃ —
May I have metta for the legless,

mettaṃ dipādakehi me
may I have metta for two-legged beings,

catuppādehi me mettaṃ —
may I have metta for four-legged creatures,

mettaṃ bahuppadehi me
may I have metta for the many-legged.

Mā maṃ apādako hiṃsi —
May the legless one not harm me,

mā maṃ hiṃsi dipādako
may the two-legged one not harm me,

mā maṃ catuppado hiṃsi —
may the four-legged one not harm me,

mā maṃ hiṃsi bahuppado;
may the many-legged one not harm me;

Sabbe sattā, sabbe pānā —
All beings, all living things,

sabbe bhutā cā kevalā
all beings and completely (all that exist),

sabbe bhadrāni passantu —
let everyone see the good (things),

mā kanci pāpamā gamā
let no one go to evil.

Appamāno buddho,
The Buddha is immeasurable,

appamāno dhammo,
the Dhamma is infinite,

appamāno sangho
the Sangha is immense.

Appamānavantāni siriṃsapāni
There are countless crawling creatures.



ahi vicchika satapadi unnānābhi sarabhū mūsika
snakes, scorpions, centipedes, spiders, wasps, mice,

katā me rakkhā katā me parittā.
my protection is created, my enclosure (paritta) is accomplished.

Paṭikkamantu bhūtāni
Let the beings (spirits) retreat

sohaṁ namo bhagavato
respect to the Blessed One,

namo sattānaṃ sammāsambuddhānanti
respect for beings who are Self-awakened (Perfect Buddhas).
