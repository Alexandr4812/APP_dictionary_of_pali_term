katamā dasa?

какие десять?

восприятие непостоянства, безличности, непривлекательности,
ādīnavasaññā, pahānasaññā, virāgasaññā,

восприятие прекращения, непривязанности/неуслаждения всем миром,

восприятие непостоянства во всех saṅkhāra,

katamā cānanda, aniccasaññā?

и что, такое восприятие непостоянства?
idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā

здесь, Ананда, монах, ушедший в лес,
rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā

или к корню дерева, или в пустое жилище,
iti paṭisañcikkhati

so he thinks:


rūpaṃ aniccaṃ, vedanā aniccā, saññā aniccā,

form is impermanent, feeling is impermanent, perception is impermanent,
saṅkhārā aniccā, viññāṇaṃ anicca’nti.

the constituents (saṅkhārā) are impermanent, and consciousness is impermanent.
iti imesu pañcasu upādānakkhandhesu

So, in these five aggregates of clinging (upādānakkhandha),
aniccānupassī viharati.

He is the observer of impermanence.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, aniccasaññā.

It's called the perception of impermanence.

katamā cānanda, anattasaññā?

and what is this perception of impersonality?


idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā

here, Ananda, a monk who has gone into the forest,
rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā

or to the root of a tree, or to an empty dwelling,
iti paṭisañcikkhati

so he thinks:

‘cakkhu anattā, rūpā anattā,

the eye is not me, the form is not me,
sotaṃ anattā, saddā anattā,

the ear is not me, the sounds are not me,
ghānaṃ anattā, gandhā anattā,

nose is not me, smells are not me,
jivhā anattā, rasā anattā,

the language is not me, the tastes are not me,
kāyo anattā, phoṭṭhabbā anattā,

the body of the non-self, the tangible non-self,
mano anattā, dhammā anattā’ti.

The mind is not me, the objects of the mind (dhammā) are not me.
iti imesu chasu ajjhattikabāhiresu āyatanesu

so, in these six inner and outer spheres (āyatana),
anattānupassī viharati.

I am the one who observes.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, anattasaññā.

This is called non-self perception.

katamā cānanda, asubhasaññā?

What is the perception of the unattractive?
idhānanda, bhikkhu imameva kāyaṃ

here the monk is this body
uddhaṃ pādatalā adho kesamatthakā

from the top to the soles, from the bottom to the top of the head,
tacapariyantaṃ

limited by the skin,
pūraṃ nānāppakārassa asucino paccavekkhati

filled with various kinds of filth, considers:

atthi imasmiṃ kāye kesā lomā nakhā dantā taco —

there are in this body: scalp hair, body hair, nails, teeth, skin,
maṃsaṃ nahāru aṭṭhi aṭṭhimiñjaṃ

meat, tendons, bones, bone marrow,
vakkaṃ hadayaṃ yakanaṃ kilomakaṃ pihakaṃ

kidneys, heart, liver, pleura, spleen,
papphāsaṃ antaṃ antaguṇaṃ

lungs, intestines, intestinal coils,
udariyaṃ karīsaṃ

what was eaten, feces,
pittaṃ semhaṃ pubbo lohitaṃ

bile, mucus, pus, blood,
sedo medo assu vasā

sweat, grease, tears, lard,
kheḷo siṅghāṇikā lasikā mutta’nti.

saliva, snot, joint lubricant, and urine.


iti imasmiṃ kāye asubhānupassī viharati.

Thus, in this body, one dwells, observing unattractiveness.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, asubhasaññā.

This is called the perception of unattractiveness.

katamā cānanda, ādīnavasaññā?

Каково восприятие опасности?
idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā

здесь, Ананда, монах, ушедший в лес,
rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā

или к корню дерева, или в пустое жилище,
iti paṭisañcikkhati

так размышляет:


bahudukkho kho ayaṃ kāyo, bahu‑ādīnavo.

это тело многострадально, полное опасностей.
iti imasmiṃ kāye vividhā ābādhā uppajjanti, seyyathidaṃ

в этом теле возникают различные болезни, а именно:


cakkhu‑rogo sota‑rogo ghāna‑rogo jivhā‑rogo

kāya‑rogo sīsa‑rogo kaṇṇa‑rogo mukha‑rogo

тела, головы, уха, рта,
danta‑rogo oṭṭha‑rogo

зубов, губ,
kāso sāso pināso ḍāho jaro

кашель, одышка, насморк, жар, старость,
kucchi‑rogo mucchā pakkhandikā sūlā visūcikā

болезни живота, обморок, эпилепсия, колика, холера,
kuṭṭhaṃ gaṇḍo kilāso soso apamāro

проказа, опухоли, белая проказа, чахотка, чума,
daddu kaṇḍu kacchu nakhasā vitacchikā lohitaṃ

лишай, зуд, чесотка, заусеницы, трещины кожи, кровотечения,
pittaṃ madhumeho aṃsā piḷakā bhagandalā

желчная болезнь, диабет (madhumeha), боли в плечах, фурункулы, свищи,


pittasamuṭṭhānā ābādhā semhasamuṭṭhānā ābādhā vātasamuṭṭhānā ābādhā sannipātikā ābādhā utupariṇāmajā ābādhā visamaparihārajā ābādhā opakkamikā ābādhā kammavipākajā ābādhā

болезни, возникшие из желчи, слизи, ветра, смешанные, от смены сезонов, от неподходящего режима, от вмешательства, от результата каммы,


sītaṃ uṇhaṃ jighacchā pipāsā uccāro passāvo’ti.

cold, heat, hunger, thirst, defecation, urination’.
iti imasmiṃ kāye ādīnavānupassī viharati.

thus contemplates the danger in this body.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, ādīnavasaññā.

this is the perception of danger.

"katamā cānanda, pahānasaññā?

What is the perception of leaving?
idhānanda, bhikkhu uppannaṃ kāmavitakkaṃ nādhivāseti,

the arising thought of sensuality does not allow,
pajahati, vinodeti, byantīkaroti, anabhāvaṃ gameti.

it leaves, expels, stops, and leads to non-occurrence.


uppannaṃ byāpādavitakkaṃ nādhivāseti, pajahati, vinodeti, byantīkaroti, anabhāvaṃ gameti.

the arising of the thought of ill-will (byāpāda-vitakka) is prevented, expelled, terminated, and brought to non-arising.

 



uppannaṃ vihiṃsāvitakkaṃ nādhivāseti, pajahati, vinodeti, byantīkaroti, anabhāvaṃ gameti.

 the arising of the thought of violence (vihiṃsā-vitakka) is prevented, expelled, terminated, and brought to non-arising.


uppannuppanne pāpake akusale dhamme nādhivāseti, pajahati, vinodeti, byantīkaroti, anabhāvaṃ gameti.
the arising-arisen bad unbeneficial phenomena (dhamma) does not allow, leaves, drives away, brings to disappearance, and leads to nonexistence.


ayaṃ vuccatānanda, pahānasaññā.

This, Ananda, is the perception of leaving.

katamā cānanda, virāgasaññā?

What is the perception of dispassion/extinction (virāga)?


idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā

here, Ananda, a monk who has gone into the forest,
rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā

or to the root of a tree, or to an empty dwelling,
iti paṭisañcikkhati

so he thinks:


etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ

this is peaceful, this is excellent:
yadidaṃ sabba‑saṅkhāra‑samatho

it is the pacification of all saṅkhāra,
sabbūpadhi‑paṭinissaggo

 dropping all support-assignments (sabbūpadhi-paṭinissaggo)

exhaustion of thirst, dispassion, nibbana’.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, virāgasaññā.

 This, Ananda, is the perception of dispassion.

katamā cānanda, nirodhasaññā?

What is the perception of termination?

 

idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā

here, Ananda, a monk who has gone into the forest,
rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā

or to the root of a tree, or to an empty dwelling,
iti paṭisañcikkhati

so he thinks:


etaṃ santaṃ etaṃ paṇītaṃ yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhippaṭinissaggo taṇhākkhayo nirodho nibbāna’nti.
this is the peaceful, this is the excellent; namely, the stilling of all compound-formations, the dropping of all propositions-appropriations, the cessation of craving, the cessation (nirodho), nibbana.

ayaṃ vuccatānanda, nirodhasaññā.
this is called, Ananda, the perception of cessation.



katamā cānanda, sabbaloke anabhiratasaññā?

What is the perception of displeasure by the whole world?


idhānanda, bhikkhu ye loke upādānā

Here, Ananda, what are the grasps in the world?
cetaso adhiṭṭhāna‑abhinivesa‑anusaya

mental attitudes, sticking, hidden tendencies
te pajahanto viharati anupādiyanto.

He lives by leaving them, not grasping at them.
ayaṃ vuccatānanda, sabbaloke anabhiratasaññā.

 It's called Ananda, the perception of non-pleasure by the entire world.



"katamā cānanda, sabbasaṅkhāresu anicchāsaññā?

What is the impermanence, Ananda, of all compound formations?
idhānanda, bhikkhu sabbasaṅkhāresu aṭṭīyati harāyati jigucchati.
here, Ananda, the monk feels disgust (aṭṭīyati), shame-disgust (harāyati), and abhorrence (jigucchati) for all compound formations


ayaṃ vuccatānanda, sabbasaṅkhāresu anicchāsaññā.
this is called, Ananda, the perception of non-desire for all composite formations.



katamā cānanda, ānāpānassati?

What is ānāpānassati?



idhānanda, bhikkhu araññagato vā rukkhamūlagato vā suññāgāragato vā nisīdati pallaṅkaṃ ābhujitvā ujuṃ kāyaṃ paṇidhāya parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā.
Here, Ananda, a monk who has gone into the forest or come to the root of a tree or come to an empty dwelling — sits cross-legged, with a straight body, with awareness in front of his mouth (parimukhaṃ satiṃ upaṭṭhapetvā).


so satova assasati satova passasati.

he inhales consciously and exhales consciously.


dīghaṃ vā assasanto ‘dīghaṃ assasāmī’ti pajānāti.
long (dīghaṃ), inhaling, “I inhale long” — so he recognizes (pajānāti).

dīghaṃ vā passasanto ‘dīghaṃ passasāmī’ti pajānāti.
long, exhaling, “long exhale” — so it recognizes.

rassaṃ vā assasanto ‘rassaṃ assasāmī’ti pajānāti.
shortly (rassaṃ), inhaling, ‘I inhale briefly’ — so it recognizes.

rassaṃ vā passasanto ‘rassaṃ passasāmī’ti pajānāti.
briefly, exhaling, ‘briefly exhale’ — so it recognizes.

sabbakāyapaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«the whole-body (breathing)-feeling (sabba-kāya-paṭisaṃvedī) will-inhale» — this is how one trains (sikkhati).

‘sabbakāyapaṭisaṃvedī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«the whole-body (breathing)-feeling will-exhale» — this is how it is trained.

‘passambhayaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“pacifying (passambhayaṃ) the bodily-activity-of-breathing (kāya-saṅkhāraṃ), I will inhale” — this is how one trains.

‘passambhayaṃ kāyasaṅkhāraṃ passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«calming the bodily-activity-of-breathing, I will exhale» — this is how one trains.

‘pītipaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«joy-feeling (pīti-paṭisaṃvedī) will-inhale» — this is how one trains.

‘pītipaṭisaṃvedī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«joy-feeling will-exhale» — this is how one trains.

‘sukhapaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«bliss-feeling (sukha-paṭisaṃvedī) will-inhale» — this is how one trains.

‘sukhapaṭisaṃvedī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“bliss-feeling will-exhale” — this is how one trains.

‘cittasaṅkhārapaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“mental-activity (citta-saṅkhāra) sensing I will inhale” — this is how one trains.

‘cittasaṅkhārapaṭisaṃvedī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«mental-activity sensing I will exhale» — this is how one trains.

‘passambhayaṃ cittasaṅkhāraṃ assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«by pacifying mental-activity I will inhale» — this is how one trains.

‘passambhayaṃ cittasaṅkhāraṃ passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“calming the mental activity, I will exhale” — this is how one trains.

‘cittapaṭisaṃvedī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«mind-feeling(citta-paṭisaṃvedī) will-inhale» — this is how it is trained.

‘cittapaṭisaṃvedī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
«mind-feeling will-exhale» — this is how one trains.

abhippamodayaṃ cittaṃ…pe… samādahaṃ cittaṃ…pe… vimocayaṃ cittaṃ…pe…
«inflaming-with-joy (abhi-ppamodayaṃ) the mind … and so on … collecting-in-samadhi (samādahaṃ) the mind … and so on … liberating (vimocayaṃ) the mind … and so on …»

aniccānupassī…pe… virāgānupassī…pe… nirodhānupassī…pe…
«contemplating-impermanence (anicca-anupassī) …

and so on ... contemplating the fading of passion (virāga-anupassī) ... and so on ... contemplating the cessation (nirodha-anupassī) ... and so on ...

‘paṭinissaggānupassī assasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“contemplating-release (paṭinissagga-anupassī) will-inhale” — this is how one trains.

‘paṭinissaggānupassī passasissāmī’ti sikkhati.
“contemplating-letting go, I will exhale” — this is how one trains.

ayaṃ vuccatānanda, ānāpānassati.
this is called, Ananda, remembering-the-breath.

"sace kho tvaṃ, ānanda, girimānandassa bhikkhuno imā dasa saññā bhāseyyāsi, ṭhānaṃ kho panetaṃ vijjati yaṃ girimānandassa bhikkhuno imā dasa saññā sutvā so ābādho ṭhānaso paṭippassambheyyā"ti.
"but if you, Ananda, were to tell this monk Girimananda these ten perceptions (dasa saññā) (bhāseyyāsi), there is reason (hhānaṃ ... vijjati) that the monk Girimananda, upon hearing these ten perceptions, would immediately (hhānaso) subside (paṭippassambheyyā)" — so [said].

atha kho āyasmā ānando bhagavato santike imā dasa saññā uggahetvā yenāyasmā girimānando tenupasaṅkami,
then the venerable Ananda, having learned these ten perceptions from the Blessed One in his presence, went to the place where the venerable Girimananda approached (upasakami).

upasaṅkamitvā āyasmato girimānandassa imā dasa saññā abhāsi.
approaching, the venerable Girimānanda, these ten perceptions were uttered.

atha kho āyasmato girimānandassa dasa saññā sutvā so ābādho ṭhānaso paṭippassambhi.
and then, when the venerable Girimānanda heard the ten perceptions, the illness immediately subsided.

vuṭṭhahi cāyasmā girimānando tamhā ābādhā.
and the venerable Girimananda rose up from that illness.

tathā pahīno ca panāyasmato girimānandassa so ābādho ahosī"ti.
and thus removed (pahīno) from the venerable Girimānanda, that disease became.
