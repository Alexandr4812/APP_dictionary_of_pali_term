Meditation on the Merits of the Buddha (Buddhānussati)
Loving-kindness (Mettā)
Repulsiveness (Asubha)
Meditation on Death (Maraṇanussati)

These are four forms of meditation that bhikkhus should not neglect. As a rule, a bhikkhu who practices them once or twice a day develops faith, becomes aware of disgust for saṃsāra, and gains support in observing monastic practices. These four types of meditation also serve as a form of protection for the bhikkhu. Therefore they are also known as “the four types of protection.” There is a series of stanzas called the “Chaturārakṣā” (Four Types of Protection) on Buddhānussati, mettā, asubha and maraṇanussati, composed by ancient teachers as an aid to memorization so that it is easy to reflect on these four objects, such as reflection on the merits of the Buddha. It has long been used by bhikkhus in Sri Lanka. This series of stanzas is very suitable for recitation as a communal meditation. Real benefit from their recitation is achieved when their meaning is studied. Therefore the series of stanzas with their translation is given below.

 

Meditation — Reflection on the Merits of the Buddha

Contents

1. "SAVĀSANE KILESE SO EKO SABBE NIGHĀTIYA,
AHŪ SUSUDDHASANTĀNO PŪJĀNANCA SADĀRAHO"
The Blessed One, having overcome all defilements together with their ingrained tendencies, attained an immeasurably pure mind. He is worthy of receiving countless offerings from devas and humans.

2. "SABBAKĀLAGATE DHAMME SABBE SAMMĀ SAYA MUNI.
SABBĀKĀRENA BUJJHITVĀ EKO SABBANNUTAM GATO".
Having comprehended by himself all Dhamma pertaining to the past, present and future, he became the supreme awakened one.

3. "VIPASSANĀDI VIJJĀHI SĪLĀDI CARANEHI CA,
SUSAMIDDHEHI SAMPANNO GAGANĀBHEHI NĀYAKO".
Filled, like the boundless sky, with the knowledge of vipassanā, with virtue and with commendable conduct, he is a leader endowed with excellent qualities.

4. "SAMMĀGATO SUBHAM THĀNAM AMOGHAVACANO CA SO,
TIVIDHASSĀ PI LOKASSA СĀTĀ NIRAVASESATO".
The Blessed One has arrived at the highest blissful attainment, and his words are unfailing. He completely penetrated the three worlds.

5. "ANEKEHI GUNОGHEHI SABBASATTUTTAMO AHU
ANEKEHI UPĀYEHI NARADAMME DAMESI CA".
The Blessed One, full of virtues and excellent in many treasures, is the best of all beings. By many skilful means he tamed untamed people.

6. "EKO SABBASSA LOKASSA SABBATTHĀNUSĀSAKO,
BHĀGYA ISSARIYĀDĪNAM GUNĀNAM PARAMO NIDHI".
The sole beneficent teacher for the whole world. A supreme treasury of wholesome qualities and other virtues.

7. "PANNĀSSA SABBADHAMMESU KARUNĀ SABBAJANTUSU,
ATTATTHĀNAM PARATTHĀNAM SĀDHIKĀ GUTAJETTHIKĀ".
The Blessed One is full of wisdom concerning all things and of compassion for all beings—these are the two foremost qualities that benefit oneself and others.

8. "DAYĀYA PĀRAMĪ CITVĀ PANNĀYATTĀNAMUDDHARĪ.
UDDHARĪ SABBADHĀMME CA DĀYĀYANNE CA UDDHARĪ."
Having perfected the pāramīs through compassion and having uplifted those devoted to wisdom, he crossed the ocean of saṃsāra. He mastered all Dhamma. In his compassion he liberated other beings from the ocean of saṃsāra.

9. "DISSAMĀNO PI TĀVASSA RŪPAKĀYO ACINTIYO,
ASĀDHĀRANANĀNADDE DHAMMAKĀYE KATHĀVA KĀ."
The Blessed One’s visible physical body is inconceivable. What then can be said of his Dhamma-body, which is endowed with incomparable wisdom?
Those who practice this meditation should read other books or learn from teachers to understand the detailed meaning of these stanzas, which have been briefly mentioned above. The recitation of these stanzas will develop faith (saddhā) only when their meaning is understood.

Meditation of Loving-kindness (Mettā-bhāvanā)

Contents

1. "SUKHĪ BHAVEYYAM NIDDUKKHO AHAM NICCAM AHAM VIYA,
HITĀ CA ME SUKHĪ HONTU MAJJHATTĀTHA CA VERINO."
May I be always well and happy! May I be free from sorrow!
And, just as I am, may all friendly beings, neutral beings, and even enemies be well and happy!

2. "IMAMHI GĀMAKKHETTAMHI SATTĀ HONTU SUKHĪ SADĀ,
TATO PARANCA RAJJESU CAKKAVĀLESU JANTUNO."
May all beings in this village always be well and happy!
May all beings in the kingdoms and world-systems beyond it always be well and happy!

3. "SAMANTĀ CAKKAVĀLESU SATTĀNANTESU PĀNINO
SUKHINO PUGGALĀ BHŪTĀ ATTABHĀVAGATĀ SIYUM"
May all living beings among the countless beings in all world-systems,
all persons, all those existing in any states of being, be happy!

4. "TATHĀ ITTHI PUMĀ CEVA ARIYĀ ANARIYĀ PI CA,
DEVĀ NARĀ APĀYATTHĀ TATHĀ DASADISĀSU CA."
Likewise, may all women and men, noble and non-noble, devas, humans and beings in the lower realms, be well and happy!

Meditation on Repulsiveness (Asubha-bhāvanā)

Contents

1. "VANNA SANTHĀNA GANDHEHI ĀSAYO KĀSATO TATHĀ,
PATIKKŪLĀNI KĀYE ME KUNAPĀNI DVĪSOLASA."
Thirty-two parts of my body, such as hair, etc., are repulsive in color, shape, smell, arrangement and outline.

2. "PATITAMHĀ PI KUNAPĀ JEGUCCHAM KĀYANISSITAM,
ĀDHĀRO HI SUCI TASSA KĀYETU KUNAPE THĪTO."
The parts that remain attached to the body are more repulsive than those that have fallen away. Why? Because the place where a part has fallen away is clean. What is on the body is in an unclean place. That is why.

3. "MĪLHE KIMIVA KĀYO YAM ASUCIMHI SAMUTTHITO,
ANTO ASUCI SAMPUNNO PUNNAVACCAKUTĪ VIYA."
This body is like a worm born in filth, arising in uncleanliness; within it it is full of uncleanness, like a latrine filled to the brim.

4. "ASUCI SANDATE NICCAM YATHĀ MEDAKATHĀLIKĀ,
NĀNĀ KIMIKULĀVĀSO PAKKACANDANIKĀ VIYA."
Unclean excretions (such as feces, urine, mucus, saliva, pus, etc.) constantly flow from this body, like from a porous pot containing raw meat. This body is like a rotting cesspit, a dwelling-place for various worms.

5. "GANDABHŪTO ROGABHŪTO VANABHŪTO SAMUSSASO,
ATEKICCHOTIJEGUCCHO PABHINNAKUNAPŪPAMO."
This body is like an abscess. Like a disease. Like a sore wound. Difficult to maintain...

Meditation on Death (Maraṇanussati)

Contents

1. "MAHĀSAMPATTISAMPATTĀ YATHĀ SATTĀ MATĀ IDHA,
TATHĀ AHAM MARISSĀMI MARANAM MAMA HESSATĪ."
I too will die, just as beings who attained great attainments have died. Death will come to me.

2. "UPPATTIYĀ SAHE VEDAM MARANAM ĀGATAM SADĀ,
MARANATTHĀYA OKĀSAM VADHAKO VIYA ESATI."
Death is something that came together with birth. Death always seeks an opportunity to kill, like an executioner.

3. "ĪSAKAM ANIVATTANTAM SATATAM GAMANUSSUKAM,
JĪVITAM UDAYĀ ATTHAM SURIYO VIYA DHĀVATI."
Life does not pause even for a moment; from birth it runs toward death, like the sun moving from rising toward setting.

4. "VIJJUBUBBULA USSĀVA JALARĀJI PARIKKHAYA,
GHĀTAKOVA RIPŪ TASSA SABATTĀPI AVĀRIYO."
This life ends as swiftly as lightning, a water-bubble, a drop of dew, or a line drawn on water. This death is like an inevitable enemy that comes to slay you.

5. "SUYASATTHĀMA PUNNIDDHI BUDDHI VUDDHI JINADVAYAM,
GHĀTESI MARANAM KHIPPAM KĀTU MĀDISAKE KATHĀ."
[Even those who possessed] great fame and accumulation of merits, wisdom and development — the two victors (the Buddha and the Paccekabuddha) —
death quickly took them away. What then to say of one like me?

6. "PACCAYĀNAСCA VEKALYA BĀHIRAJJHATTUPADDAVĀ,
MARĀMORAM NIMESĀPI MARAMĀNO ANUKKHANAM."
I, who die every moment due to the disintegration of nāma-rūpa, will die faster than the blink of an age, because of the lack of necessary conditions (for life) or from an external danger.

Contents

This meditation on reflection on death is also profound. This practice should be done after learning the details from a teacher. The particulars of these practices should be studied, at minimum, from traditional meditation manuals.
